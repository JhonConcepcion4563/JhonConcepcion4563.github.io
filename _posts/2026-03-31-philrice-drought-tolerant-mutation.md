Our research at PhilRice developed drought-tolerant rice lines through induced mutation — offering new hope for farmers in rainfed areas affected by water scarcity.

The improved lines were developed from the upland variety NSIC Rc 9 (Apo) using in vitro mutagenesis, which combines tissue culture with gamma irradiation to induce beneficial mutations. Five drought-screened lines were identified, performing well under moderate to severe drought with average yields higher than the national average. These lines showed improvements not just in drought resilience but also in grain shape, length, and overall eating quality.

This mutation breeding approach also led to the release of new varieties suitable for rainfed, drought-prone areas, helping address significant losses that Filipino farmers face during droughts.
