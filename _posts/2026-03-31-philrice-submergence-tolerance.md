During my time as a Science Research Analyst at the Philippine Rice Research Institute (PhilRice), our team developed new rice breeding lines with high submergence tolerance — a critical advancement for flood-prone farming areas in the Philippines.

The breeding lines, such as NSIC Rc160-Sub1, exhibited a 90–95% survival rate under submerged conditions, a marked improvement compared to earlier trials. The research leveraged marker-assisted selection and used IR64-Sub1 as the submergence tolerance donor. The development of these lines is crucial for improving rice resilience in flood-prone areas, supporting food security under climate variability.
