Michigan State University's College of Agriculture and Natural Resources featured me as part of their "Fresh Crop of New Scientists" — highlighting my PhD completion in Plant Breeding, Genetics, and Biotechnology.

The article reflects on my growth as a researcher, including the milestone of publishing my first first-author paper, which identified a significant SNP and candidate gene for low Deoxynivalenol (DON) content in wheat using hyperspectral imaging data. Prior to my PhD, I had no experience with hyperspectral imaging or GWAS, making this accomplishment especially meaningful.

Outside of research, I served as Chair of the Graduate Student Working Group (2023–2024) of the National Association of Plant Breeders. Following my PhD, I joined Dr. Davis Lowry's team in MSU's Department of Plant Biology as a Postdoctoral Research Associate, focusing on phenomics and high-throughput phenotyping with switchgrass.
